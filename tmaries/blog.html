JUNE RECIPE WINNER

Strawberry Cupcakes by Kelly G. in Wisconsin

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of fresh strawberries
  • 1/4 Whole mild
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 10 tablespoons of butter, room temp
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs

Frosting

  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 3 1/2 Cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 3 tsp milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh strawberries chopped (to garnish)

Step 1

  • Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Line muffin tins with 20 cupcake liners (for mini cupcakes, line 72 mini muffin tins).

Step 2

  • Grind strawberries into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or food processor. Whisk strawberry powder, all-purpose flour, cake flour, baking powder, bakingsoda, and salt together in a bowl.

Step 3

  • Beat butter and sugar in a bowl with an electric mixer until light and fluffy. Mixture should be noticeably lighter in color. Add room-temperature eggs one at a time, allowing each egg to blend into butter mixture before adding the next. Beat in vanilla extract.

Step 4

  • Stir flour mixture, alternately with the milk, into butter mixture until just incorporated. Fill lined tins with cupcake batter.

Step 5

  • Bake in preheated oven until cupcakes spring back when gently touched with a fingertip or a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, 16 to 18 minutes for regular cupcakes, 8 to 10 minutes for mini cupcakes.

How to Pipe Scalloped Edging

The optimum tip for piping the scales is a large round tip, however I frequently use a piping bag with the tip chopped off for this purpose. Position the bag so that the seams are on the side of the cake rather than in the line of sight this will save time. While the frosting is pliable or soft, place the spoon at an angle on the bottom edge of the cake. Press gently and drag it parallel along with the cake, creating a 2-inch-long horizontal line in the frosting. Repeat, overlapping a little each time with the previous line, moving around the cake. Now move the spoon up by 1 inch and repeat to form another scalloped pattern above the last one; repeat until you've reached the top of the cake. Return the cake to the refrigerator for half an hour to firm up the frosting before serving. To create the two-tone look, fill one side of the piping bag or alternate dots with one color of buttercream and the other with another dot of buttercream, alternating between the two colors. Colors that are very similar should be combined for the greatest impact. Set aside a little amount of uncolored buttercream. You may use the conserved white to brighten any of the colored batches, or you can mix them together.

Salted Caramel Buttercream Chocolate Cupcakes

These cupcakes combine a rich, moist chocolate cupcakes with an easy salted caramel filling and salted caramel buttercream. This is the best kind of indulgence.

  • 1 cup all purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cocoa
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup white granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup dark brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs, room temp
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk

For the Salted Caramel Drizzle

  • 9 oz. store bought chewy caramel
  • 1/2 cup thickened heavy cream
  • 1 tsp sea salt flakes

For Salted Caramel Buttercream

  • 1 stick of unsalted butter, room temp
  • 2 oz dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup salted caramel
  • 2 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 1/2 tsp finely ground salt
